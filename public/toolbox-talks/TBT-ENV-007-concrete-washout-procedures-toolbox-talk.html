Concrete Washout Procedures

Toolbox Talk Record

Ref: TBT-ENV-007  |  Issue: 1  |  Date: March 2026
PresenterProject
LocationDate

What?

  • Concrete washout is the water used to clean concrete mixers, pumps, chutes, and tools after placing operations.
  • Washout water is highly alkaline with a pH above 12, containing cement particles, sand, and chemical admixtures.
  • Discharging washout water into drains, watercourses, or onto unprotected ground is a criminal offence under pollution regulations.
  • A designated washout area with an impermeable lined containment system must be established before any concrete work begins.
  • The washout area must be clearly signed and located away from site drainage, watercourses, and ecologically sensitive areas.
  • Concrete mixer trucks must discharge their washout water only into the designated containment facility, not onto bare ground.
  • Solidified washout residue must be removed and disposed of as construction waste through the correct waste stream.
  • Pump pipeline cleanout water must also be directed to the containment system — it contains the same pollutants as mixer washout.
  • The Environment Agency can issue unlimited fines and stop-work notices for concrete washout entering any watercourse.
  • Every person involved in concrete operations must know the washout location and procedure before the pour begins.

Why?

Water pollutionConcrete washout kills aquatic life rapidly — its extreme alkalinity and suspended solids destroy river ecosystems for miles downstream.
Unlimited finesPolluting a watercourse with concrete washout is a criminal offence carrying unlimited fines and potential imprisonment.
Simple preventionA lined containment area and clear signing prevent all concrete washout pollution — the solution is cheap and straightforward.
Do Don't
  • Establish a designated, impermeable-lined washout area before any concrete work begins.
  • Sign the washout area clearly and brief all concrete workers on its location.
  • Direct all concrete mixer truck washout to the designated containment facility only.
  • Contain pump pipeline cleanout water in the same system as mixer washout.
  • Locate the washout area away from site drains, watercourses, and sensitive habitats.
  • Remove solidified washout residue regularly and dispose of it as construction waste.
  • Inspect the washout containment for leaks and capacity before each pour day.
  • Brief delivery drivers on the washout procedure and location at the site entrance.
  • Monitor nearby drains for any signs of concrete contamination during pour operations.
  • Report any washout spill or containment failure to the environmental manager immediately.
  • DON'T discharge concrete washout water into drains, ditches, or watercourses.
  • DON'T wash out concrete equipment onto bare ground without impermeable containment.
  • DON'T begin concrete operations without a designated washout area established on site.
  • DON'T allow delivery drivers to wash out in undesignated areas of the site.
  • DON'T locate washout areas near site drainage or ecologically sensitive features.
  • DON'T let solidified washout residue overflow the containment — clear it regularly.
  • DON'T use a washout facility that is leaking or near capacity without repair.
  • DON'T assume drivers know the procedure — brief them at the entrance every time.
  • DON'T ignore milky or discoloured water in nearby drains — investigate the source.
  • DON'T delay reporting a washout spill — early containment limits environmental damage.
