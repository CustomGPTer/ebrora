Scissor Lift Safety

Ref: TBT-WAH-008  |  Issue: 1  |  Date: March 2026
PresenterProject
LocationDate

What?

  • A scissor lift is a mobile elevating work platform that raises a flat working platform vertically using a scissor mechanism.
  • Scissor lifts are only designed for vertical elevation — they must not be driven at height unless the manufacturer permits it.
  • Overturning is the primary fatal hazard, caused by operating on slopes, uneven ground, potholes, or near excavation edges.
  • All four wheels must be on firm, level ground before elevating the platform — outriggers must be used where the model requires.
  • Operators must hold a valid IPAF PAL card or CPCS/NPORS MEWP category for the scissor lift type before operating.
  • Guardrails, mid-rails, toe boards, and entry gates on the platform must be intact and latched before elevation.
  • The maximum safe working load includes the combined weight of people, tools, and materials on the platform.
  • Wind speed affects scissor lift stability, particularly at full extension — work must stop when the manufacturer's limit is reached.
  • Crushing between the platform and overhead structures is a serious hazard — maintain awareness of clearance above at all times.
  • A rescue plan must be in place before elevation, covering how to recover a worker if the platform controls fail.

Why?

OverturnScissor lifts have a narrow base relative to platform height — slopes, potholes, and soft ground cause fatal overturns.
Crush riskThe platform rising into overhead structures traps and crushes the operator — constant awareness of overhead clearance is essential.
Not for driving at heightUnlike boom lifts, most scissor lifts must not be driven with the platform elevated — this causes instability and tip-over.
Do Don't
  • Hold a valid IPAF, CPCS, or NPORS MEWP card before operating any scissor lift.
  • Set up on firm, level ground with all wheels on solid surface before elevating.
  • Check guardrails, toe boards, and entry gates are intact and latched before use.
  • Verify the total load on the platform does not exceed the safe working load.
  • Lower the platform fully before driving to a new position unless approved to drive elevated.
  • Monitor wind speed and stop work when conditions exceed the manufacturer's limit.
  • Maintain overhead clearance awareness throughout all elevated work operations.
  • Ensure a rescue plan is in place before elevating the platform.
  • Complete the daily pre-use inspection and record the results before operating.
  • Keep the platform floor clear of trip hazards and loose materials at all times.
  • DON'T operate a scissor lift without a valid MEWP operator card.
  • DON'T elevate on slopes, soft ground, potholes, or near excavation edges.
  • DON'T use the lift with damaged guardrails, missing toe boards, or unlatched gates.
  • DON'T overload the platform beyond the stated safe working load capacity.
  • DON'T drive at height unless the specific model is designed and approved for it.
  • DON'T continue working in wind speeds above the manufacturer's rated limit.
  • DON'T raise the platform without checking for overhead structures and obstructions.
  • DON'T elevate without a rescue plan ready for platform control failure.
  • DON'T skip the daily pre-use check — faults develop overnight and between shifts.
  • DON'T stand on guardrails, lean over edges, or use ladders on the platform.
