Trench Collapse Prevention Measures

🏷 Ref: TBT-EXC-022

Trench collapses happen without warning and can bury a person in seconds. One cubic metre of soil weighs over one tonne, making even a shallow collapse potentially fatal. Ground conditions, weather, vibration from plant, and surcharge loading all affect trench stability. Every excavation must be assessed and supported before anyone enters, regardless of how short the task may be.

Key Hazards
Sudden sidewall collapse burying operatives, causing crushing injuries or suffocation
Undermining of adjacent roads, structures, or services due to unsupported excavations
Water ingress weakening trench walls and rapidly changing ground stability
Surcharge loads from spoil heaps, plant, or materials placed too close to edges
Control Measures
  • Ensure a temporary works design is in place for all excavations that require support systems.
  • Install trench sheets, shoring, or trench boxes before any person enters the excavation.
  • Keep spoil, materials, and plant at least one metre back from all excavation edges.
  • Ensure a competent person inspects the excavation at the start of every shift and after heavy rainfall.
  • Provide safe access and egress using ladders or steps within the excavation at regular intervals.
  • Monitor weather forecasts and stop work if heavy rain, frost, or thaw could affect stability.
  • Use stop blocks or barriers to prevent vehicles tracking too close to trench edges.
  • Check for buried services before excavation using service drawings and a cable avoidance tool.
  • Ensure edge protection barriers and warning signage are in place around all open excavations.
  • Report any signs of ground movement, cracking, or water seepage to the supervisor immediately.
Emergency / Rescue

If a collapse occurs, do not enter the trench to rescue. Call emergency services immediately and activate the site emergency plan. Use long-reach equipment only if safe to do so. Keep the area clear and account for all personnel.

Remember
  • Unsupported trenches can collapse without any visible warning signs.
  • One cubic metre of soil weighs over one tonne.
  • Never enter an excavation that has not been inspected today.
  • Keep all spoil and loads at least one metre from edges.
  • Conditions change — rain, frost, and vibration all reduce stability.
  • If it looks unsafe, stop work and speak to your supervisor.
Applicable Legislation: CDM 2015 (Schedule 3) · Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 · Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 · BS 6031 Code of Practice for Earthworks
