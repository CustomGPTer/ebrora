Principal Contractor Safety Duties

TBT-CDM-021

Under CDM 2015, the Principal Contractor has overall responsibility for managing health and safety during the construction phase. They must plan, manage, and monitor the work to ensure it is carried out safely, coordinate the activities of all contractors on site, and ensure the workforce is properly consulted and informed. This talk explains the key duties so every worker understands who is responsible for what on site.

Key Hazards
Poor coordination between trades leading to overlapping high-risk activities
Workers unaware of site rules due to inadequate induction or communication
Failure to manage shared areas creating uncontrolled hazards for all trades
Subcontractors working without adequate risk assessments or method statements
Control Measures
  • Ensure the Principal Contractor is formally appointed in writing before the construction phase begins.
  • Prepare and maintain a construction phase plan covering site rules, risks, and emergency arrangements.
  • Provide a site induction for every worker before they start work on the project.
  • Coordinate the activities of all contractors to prevent interference between overlapping operations.
  • Ensure all contractors provide suitable risk assessments and method statements before starting work.
  • Display site rules, emergency contacts, and the fire plan on notice boards at the site entrance.
  • Consult the workforce on health and safety matters through regular toolbox talks and safety forums.
  • Monitor contractor compliance with site rules through inspections, audits, and observations.
  • Ensure welfare facilities including toilets, washing, rest areas, and drinking water are maintained.
Remember
  • The Principal Contractor must be appointed in writing before the construction phase begins
  • A construction phase plan must be prepared and kept up to date throughout the project
  • Every worker on site must receive a site induction before they start any work activities
  • All contractors must provide suitable risk assessments and method statements before starting
  • The Principal Contractor must coordinate activities to prevent trades from creating risks for others
  • Welfare facilities must be provided and maintained to an adequate standard at all times
Applicable Legislation: CDM Regulations 2015 · Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 · Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
