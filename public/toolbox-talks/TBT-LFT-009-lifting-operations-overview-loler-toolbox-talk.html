Lifting Operations Overview (LOLER)

🏷 Ref: TBT-LFT-009

Lifting operations are among the highest-risk activities on any construction site. A dropped load can cause fatal injuries, major structural damage, and service strikes. The Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations 1998 require every lift to be properly planned, supervised, and carried out by competent people using suitable equipment. No lift should ever proceed without these controls firmly in place.

Key Hazards
Dropped loads striking personnel due to equipment failure or incorrect slinging
Crane or lifting equipment overturning from overloading or unstable ground conditions
Crushing injuries from uncontrolled load swing or trapping against structures
Contact with overhead power lines during crane or telehandler operations
Control Measures
  • Ensure every lifting operation has a written lift plan prepared by a competent appointed person.
  • Verify that all lifting equipment and accessories have current thorough examination certificates.
  • Confirm the load weight before any lift and check it is within the safe working load of equipment.
  • Use only trained and competent slingers and signallers who hold recognised qualifications.
  • Establish exclusion zones beneath and around all lifting operations to keep people clear.
  • Conduct a pre-lift briefing so all personnel understand the lift sequence and their roles.
  • Check ground conditions to confirm they can support the crane or lifting equipment being used.
  • Monitor wind speeds and stop lifting when conditions exceed the limits in the lift plan.
  • Use tag lines to control load rotation and prevent uncontrolled swinging during the lift.
  • Report any defects in lifting equipment or accessories and remove them from service immediately.
Remember
  • Every lift must be planned, supervised, and carried out by competent people.
  • Never exceed the safe working load of any lifting equipment.
  • Stay out of exclusion zones — never walk under a suspended load.
  • All lifting accessories must have valid thorough examination reports.
  • If conditions change, stop the lift and reassess before continuing.
  • Report all defects, near misses, and unsafe practices immediately.
Applicable Legislation: Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations 1998 (LOLER) · PUWER 1998 · CDM 2015 · BS 7121 Code of Practice for Safe Use of Cranes
