Falls From Height Awareness

🏷 Ref: TBT-WAH-009

Falls from height remain the single biggest killer on UK construction sites. Under the Work at Height Regulations 2005, any place where a person could fall a distance liable to cause personal injury counts as working at height. This includes ground-level work near open edges, voids, and fragile surfaces. Every operative must understand the risks and the controls that prevent fatal and life-changing injuries.

Key Hazards
Fatal or serious injury from unprotected falls at open edges and voids
Falls through fragile surfaces such as roof lights, fibre cement sheets, or liner panels
Collapse of poorly erected or overloaded access equipment and platforms
Objects falling from height striking workers below the work area
Control Measures
  • Avoid work at height wherever possible by designing the task to be done from ground level.
  • Use collective protection such as edge protection, guard rails, and safety nets before personal fall protection.
  • Ensure all open edges, floor openings, and voids are protected with rigid barriers or secured covers.
  • Inspect all access equipment before each use and confirm it is suitable for the task.
  • Check that operatives working at height hold the required training and competency for the task.
  • Secure all tools and materials to prevent them falling and striking people below.
  • Establish exclusion zones beneath overhead work areas and use signage to warn others.
  • Review weather conditions before starting height work — stop if wind, rain, or ice creates risk.
  • Report any defective edge protection, access equipment, or missing covers immediately.
Emergency / Rescue

If a fall occurs, call emergency services immediately and do not move the casualty unless they face further danger. Activate the site emergency plan and ensure a first aider attends. If someone is suspended in a harness, rescue within minutes to prevent suspension trauma.

Remember
  • Falls from height are the leading cause of death in construction.
  • Always follow the hierarchy: avoid, prevent, then mitigate falls.
  • Never remove or alter edge protection without authorisation.
  • Fragile surfaces must be clearly marked and protected at all times.
  • If conditions change, stop work and reassess before continuing.
  • Report all near misses involving work at height immediately.
Applicable Legislation: Work at Height Regulations 2005 · CDM 2015 · Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999 · Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
