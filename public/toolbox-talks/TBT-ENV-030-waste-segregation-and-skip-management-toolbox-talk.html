Waste Segregation and Skip Management

TBT-ENV-030

Proper waste segregation on construction sites reduces environmental harm, cuts disposal costs, and is a legal requirement. Mixing different waste types contaminates recyclable materials and can result in the entire skip being classified as hazardous waste at significantly higher disposal cost. Every operative must understand which waste goes into which skip and why segregation matters. Good waste management is also essential for maintaining a tidy, safe site with reduced fire and trip hazards.

Key Hazards
Environmental pollution from incorrectly disposed hazardous waste materials
Increased disposal costs from contaminated skips being classified as hazardous waste
Fire risk from mixed combustible and chemical waste stored in the same container
Manual handling injuries from overloaded skips and poor waste handling practices
Control Measures
  • Segregate waste into the designated skips for timber, metal, inert, mixed, and hazardous materials.
  • Read the skip labels and ask your supervisor if you are unsure which skip to use for any material.
  • Never place hazardous materials such as paint, solvents, or chemicals in general waste skips.
  • Keep skip areas clean, accessible, and clearly signed with the accepted waste types displayed.
  • Do not overfill skips beyond the fill line and report full skips for collection promptly.
  • Handle waste materials carefully to avoid cuts from sharp edges, nails, and broken glass.
  • Complete waste transfer notes accurately for every skip leaving the site.
  • Reduce waste generation at source by cutting materials accurately and reusing where possible.
  • Ensure hazardous waste is stored in locked, bunded, and labelled containers separate from general waste.
Remember
  • Waste segregation is a legal requirement and saves money by keeping recyclable materials clean.
  • One contaminated item can reclassify an entire skip as hazardous waste at much higher disposal cost.
  • Always check the skip label before disposing of any material and ask if you are not sure.
  • Hazardous waste must be stored separately in locked, labelled, and bunded containers.
  • Overfilled skips are a safety hazard and must not be loaded above the designated fill line.
  • Good waste management keeps the site tidy, reduces fire risk, and demonstrates environmental compliance.
Applicable Legislation: Environmental Protection Act 1990 · Waste (England and Wales) Regulations 2011 · Hazardous Waste Regulations 2005 · CDM Regulations 2015
