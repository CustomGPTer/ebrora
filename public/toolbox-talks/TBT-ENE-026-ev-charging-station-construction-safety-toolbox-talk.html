EV Charging Station Construction Safety

TBT-ENE-026

The rapid growth of electric vehicle infrastructure means construction teams are increasingly installing EV charging stations in car parks, forecourts, and highway service areas. These projects involve significant electrical work, cable trenching, and working in areas open to public traffic. This talk covers the key safety considerations for EV charging station construction on UK sites.

Key Hazards
Electric shock during cable installation and charger connection work
Struck-by risks from public vehicles in operational car parks
Cable strike when trenching through existing service corridors
Manual handling injuries from heavy charger units and transformers
Control Measures
  • Isolate and lock off all electrical supplies before starting connection work on charger units.
  • Establish a safe working zone with barriers and signage in public car parks and forecourts.
  • Complete a cable avoidance survey using CAT and Genny before excavating any cable trenches.
  • Use mechanical lifting aids for heavy charger pedestals, feeder pillars, and transformer units.
  • Ensure all electrical work is carried out by competent electricians to BS 7671 standards.
  • Brief operatives on the traffic management arrangements for working in public access areas.
  • Test and commission all charger installations before removing barriers and opening to public use.
  • Verify earthing and bonding arrangements comply with the design specification before energising.
  • Coordinate with the distribution network operator for any new or upgraded supply connections.
Remember
  • Isolate and lock off all electrical supplies before carrying out any connection work
  • Establish a safe working zone with barriers when working in public car parks
  • Complete cable avoidance surveys before trenching to prevent buried service strikes
  • All electrical installation work must comply with BS 7671 wiring regulations
  • Test and commission every charger fully before opening to public use
Applicable Legislation: Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 · BS 7671 (IET Wiring Regulations) · CDM 2015 · New Roads and Street Works Act 1991
