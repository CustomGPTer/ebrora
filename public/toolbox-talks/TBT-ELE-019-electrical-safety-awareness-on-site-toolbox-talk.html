Electrical Safety Awareness on Site

🏷 Ref: TBT-ELE-019

Electricity can kill or cause severe burns in an instant. On construction sites, temporary supplies, damaged cables, wet conditions, and contact with overhead or underground services all create serious electrical risks. Even 230 volts domestic supply is enough to be fatal. Every operative must understand basic electrical dangers and know that only competent, authorised persons may carry out electrical work.

Key Hazards
Electrocution from contact with live conductors, cables, or exposed wiring
Severe electrical burns from arc flash or passage of current through the body
Fire or explosion caused by electrical faults, overloaded circuits, or damaged equipment
Contact with overhead power lines by plant, equipment, scaffolding, or materials
Control Measures
  • Use 110 volt reduced voltage supply for all portable tools and equipment on site wherever practicable.
  • Ensure all electrical equipment is inspected and tested with a current PAT label before use.
  • Check cables, plugs, and connections for visible damage before plugging in any equipment.
  • Use residual current devices on all circuits supplying portable equipment as additional protection.
  • Never carry out electrical work unless you are a competent and authorised person.
  • Maintain safe distances from overhead power lines using goal posts, barriers, and warning signs.
  • Report any damaged cables, exposed wiring, or tripped circuits to the site electrician immediately.
  • Keep electrical equipment dry and never use it in standing water or heavy rain without protection.
  • Ensure temporary distribution boards are secured, weatherproof, and regularly inspected.
Emergency / Rescue

If someone receives an electric shock, do not touch them while they may still be in contact with the supply. Isolate the power if safe to do so, call emergency services, and begin CPR if the casualty is not breathing. Treat all electrical burns as serious.

Remember
  • Electricity can kill instantly — even 230 volts is potentially fatal.
  • Only competent, authorised persons may carry out electrical work.
  • Always use 110 volt tools on site wherever possible.
  • Visually check all cables and plugs before every use.
  • Never ignore a tripped RCD — it has operated for a reason.
  • Report all electrical faults and damaged equipment immediately.
Applicable Legislation: Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 · Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 · PUWER 1998 · BS 7671 (IET Wiring Regulations)
