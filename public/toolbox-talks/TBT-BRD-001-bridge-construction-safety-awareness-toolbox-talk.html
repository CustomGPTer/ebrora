Bridge Construction Safety Awareness

Toolbox Talk Record

Ref: TBT-BRD-001  |  Issue: 1  |  Date: March 2026
PresenterProject
LocationDate

What?

  • Bridge construction combines working at height, heavy lifting, temporary works, and traffic management into a single operation.
  • Falls from bridge decks, abutments, and falsework are a primary cause of serious injuries during bridge construction.
  • Temporary works including falsework, propping, and temporary bearings are critical to structural stability during construction.
  • Precast beam installation involves complex lifting operations with heavy loads, requiring detailed lift plans under LOLER.
  • Working over or adjacent to live traffic, railways, or waterways adds significant additional hazard to bridge construction.
  • Formwork for in-situ bridge decks must be designed, checked, and inspected with a permit to load before concrete is poured.
  • Post-tensioning operations involve high-tension cables and specialist equipment that can cause catastrophic injury if they fail.
  • Bridge bearing installation requires precision placement of heavy components in confined spaces at height.
  • Waterproofing, expansion joints, and parapet construction involve working at the exposed edges of completed bridge decks.
  • Coordination between the permanent works designer and the temporary works coordinator is essential for structural safety.

Why?

Prevent fatal fallsBridge decks, abutments, and falsework create exposed working at height — edge protection and harness systems are essential throughout.
Temporary works failureFalsework collapse during bridge construction causes catastrophic structural failure — proper design, checking, and inspection are mandatory.
Complex liftingPrecast beams and steel girders are among the heaviest single lifts on construction sites — detailed planning prevents dropped loads.
Do Don't
  • Install edge protection on all bridge decks, abutments, and elevated work areas.
  • Ensure falsework and formwork have a valid design check and permit to load.
  • Follow the approved lift plan for all beam and girder installation operations.
  • Maintain exclusion zones beneath bridge construction areas to protect workers below.
  • Coordinate with highways or railway authorities when working over live infrastructure.
  • Use harness and lanyard systems where edge protection cannot be installed at leading edges.
  • Brief all workers on the bridge construction sequence and phasing each day.
  • Monitor temporary works for movement, deflection, or signs of distress during construction.
  • Follow post-tensioning safe working procedures and maintain exclusion zones during stressing.
  • Ensure permanent and temporary works designers coordinate on stability at every stage.
  • DON'T work on bridge decks or abutments without edge protection or fall arrest systems.
  • DON'T load falsework before the design check is complete and the permit is issued.
  • DON'T lift beams or girders without a detailed lift plan briefed to all involved.
  • DON'T work beneath bridge construction without exclusion zones to protect from falling objects.
  • DON'T work over live traffic or railways without the required protection and coordination.
  • DON'T rely solely on harnesses where collective edge protection could be installed instead.
  • DON'T skip daily briefings — bridge construction hazards change with each phase of work.
  • DON'T ignore signs of falsework distress such as deflection, creaking, or misalignment.
  • DON'T enter the exclusion zone during post-tensioning stressing operations.
  • DON'T proceed with construction if there are unresolved queries between designers.
